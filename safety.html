CRITICAL WARNING: The Event Horizon

Figure 2: A NASA simulation showing the Event Horizon (black center) and the Photon Ring.

The Event Horizon is the point of no return. It is the boundary around a black hole beyond which nothing, not even light, can escape. The radius of this boundary is known as the Schwarzschild Radius ($R_s$), which depends entirely on the mass of the black hole.

DANGER: Do not cross this line. Once you pass the Event Horizon, the escape velocity exceeds the speed of light ($c$). Since nothing can travel faster than light, you will be unable to send a distress signal or return home.

Hazard: Spaghettification

Figure 3: An artist's impression of a star being ripped apart by tidal forces (ESO/M. Kornmesser).

As you get closer to the singularity (the center), the difference in gravitational pull between your feet and your head becomes extreme. This is due to Tidal Forces, which grow stronger as you get closer to the source of gravity (following an inverse-cube law).

This force will stretch you vertically and compress you horizontally. In the industry, we call this Spaghettification. It is exactly as painful as it sounds.

Safety Recommendation: Stick to Supermassive Black Holes if you want to cross the horizon alive. The tidal forces are weaker at the horizon of a supermassive black hole compared to a smaller stellar-mass black hole. (Though you still can't come back!)
