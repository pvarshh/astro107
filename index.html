Choose Your Destination:

Not all black holes are created equal! Before you book, know the difference:

  • Stellar-Mass Black Holes: Formed from the collapse of a massive star. Small (only ~10-100 miles across) but deadly. Warning: Extreme tidal forces will spaghettify you before you even reach the horizon.
  • Supermassive Black Holes: Found at the center of galaxies (like Sagittarius A* in the Milky Way). Millions of miles across. "Safe" to cross the horizon alive (but you can never leave).
