Time Dilation Essentials

Figure 6: Time runs slower closer to the mass. Your watch vs. Earth's watch.

Due to the intense gravitational field, time passes slower near a black hole compared to empty space. This is known as Gravitational Time Dilation.

This effect is predicted by Einstein's Theory of General Relativity. The closer you are to a massive object, the slower time moves for you relative to an observer far away.

Schedule Alert: A few hours spent sightseeing near the Event Horizon could mean decades have passed on Earth. This is often called the "Twin Paradox" scenarioâ€”if you leave your twin on Earth, you will return younger than them!

Packing List:

  • A Good Book (or 100): If you are waiting for a rescue that will never come, you'll have plenty of time.
  • Tidal Force Resistant Suit: Essential to delay spaghettification (results may vary).
  • No Watch Needed: Your watch will tick slower than your friends' watches back on Earth. Bringing one just makes the math confusing.
  • Redshift Filter: Light climbing out of the gravity well loses energy and becomes "redder" (Gravitational Redshift). Bring a filter to correct your photos!
